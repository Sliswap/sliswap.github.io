**Privacy Policy**

*Last updated: 2025.8.8*

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how **Sliswap** ("we," "our," "us," or "the Platform") collects, uses, shares, and protects information when you use our decentralized exchange ("Sliswap") and related services. We comply with applicable privacy and data protection laws, including the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**, the **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)**, and other relevant international regulations.

## 1. Scope of This Policy

This Policy applies to all interactions with our Sliswap platform, including:

- Accessing the Sliswap through a browser, mobile application, or API
- Connecting your cryptocurrency wallet to our interface
- Participating in swaps, liquidity pools, staking, governance, or other smart contract interactions

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 On-Chain Data (Automatically Collected)

- Public wallet address(es) connected to the Sliswap
- Blockchain transactions, token addresses, and amounts
- Smart contract calls and chain IDs

### 2.2 Off-Chain Data (Collected via Interface)

- Device and browser type, operating system, screen resolution
- IP address, time zone, and approximate geolocation
- Access times, referral URLs, clickstream data
- Cookies and similar technologies

### 2.3 Voluntarily Provided Data

- Email address, support tickets, or feedback forms
- KYC/KYB data (only if required for regulatory compliance)

## 3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

We process your information under the following legal bases:

- **Consent**: For cookies, analytics, and marketing communications
- **Performance of a contract**: To provide core Sliswap functionality you request
- **Legal obligation**: To comply with anti-money laundering (AML) and counter-terrorism financing (CTF) regulations
- **Legitimate interests**: For security, fraud prevention, and platform improvement

## 4. How We Use Your Information

- To operate and improve our Sliswap
- To provide compatibility with your device and wallet
- To monitor and secure the platform against abuse
- To comply with regulatory requirements (e.g., AML/KYC, sanctions screening)
- To analyze anonymized usage trends for service optimization

## 5. Your Rights

### Under GDPR (EU/EEA Users)

You have the right to:

- Access and obtain a copy of your personal data
- Request correction of inaccurate data
- Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
- Object to certain types of processing
- Request data portability to another provider
- Withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior processing

To exercise these rights, contact us at **support@example.com**. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

### Under CCPA (California Users)

You have the right to:

- Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected about you
- Request deletion of your personal information
- Opt out of the "sale" of your personal information (we do not sell personal data)
- Non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

California residents can submit requests via **web form / email**.

## 6. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share limited data:

- **With service providers** (e.g., hosting, analytics, cloud storage) under strict confidentiality agreements
- **For legal compliance** with regulators, courts, or applicable laws
- **During business changes** such as mergers, acquisitions, or restructuring (subject to safeguards)

## 7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tools for:

- Saving your preferences (wallet connection, language, theme)
- Measuring platform usage (with privacy-friendly analytics)
- Ensuring security and fraud prevention

You can manage or disable cookies in your browser settings.

## 8. International Data Transfers

We may transfer your information to servers outside your country, including jurisdictions that may not offer the same level of protection. In such cases, we use appropriate safeguards, such as:

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) under GDPR
- Binding corporate rules
- Other legal transfer mechanisms recognized by applicable laws

## 9. Data Retention

We retain off-chain personal data only as long as necessary for:

- The purposes outlined in this Policy
- Compliance with legal obligations
- Resolution of disputes and enforcement of agreements

On-chain data is stored permanently on public blockchains and is beyond our control.

## 10. Security Measures

We employ industry-standard measures to protect off-chain data, including:

- Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS)
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security audits

However, you are solely responsible for safeguarding your wallet private keys and seed phrases. We will never request them.

## 11. Third-Party Services

Our Sliswap may link to or integrate with third-party wallets, liquidity providers, or analytics services. These third parties operate under their own privacy policies, which we encourage you to review.

## 12. Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for users under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect data from minors.

## 13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy periodically. The updated version will be posted with a revised "Last updated" date. Material changes will be announced via our website or community channels.
